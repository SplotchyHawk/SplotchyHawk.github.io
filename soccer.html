Biography

I have been an avid soccer player since the age of five. For my youth years I played mostly in the Metro league, beginning with Vancouver’s team and then to Burnaby with an increasing amount of success as time went on. In 2016 I won gold at the youth provincial A cup with the 1999 Burnaby district metro select squad. Then in 2018 went on to win bronze with the 2000 squad of the same league. I also won gold at the Surrey mayor’s cup with the same 2000 squad that same season. Throughout these years I always excelled in speed and endurance. I then decided to go to Western University of Ontario where I tried out for their varsity team. Out of approximately 100 people I was one of 7 asked to continue playing and practicing with the team. Unfortunately, about two weeks into consistent practices and fitness we were informed that the roster was full.

I did my bachelor's in computer science with a minor in game development and because of the heavy course load decided to take a break from high intensity soccer for second year and played indoor and outdoor leagues. Third and fourth year soccer was disrupted by Covid but I played competitively during summers in Vancouver where Covid regulations were less strict.

I was referred to a New Westminister summer team where I dominated and helped them to a league victory with an average of 2 goals per game. I needed more competitiveness and therefore, after graduating from university in June of 2022, tried out for Juve FC in BMSL Div 1 which is where I have been playing for the past year. I am currently their top goal scorer. I excel in speed intensive positions like striker or winger. I gravitate towards striker as I can conserve more energy to give my all on runs, finish consistently, and be that outlet up top.
